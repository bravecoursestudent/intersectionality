Intersectionality

Creating Equal Social Systems





Intersectionality is the overlapping social identity of people and related systems of oppression, domination, and discrimination. These systems interlock to form bias that greatly effects individuals. This web site identifies some of these social problems and provides

Race

  • Reparations to repair harms of racial slavery, Jim Crow, mass incarceration, and other systems of oppression.
  • Reparations for displacement of Indigenous people.
  • Affordable fair housing to reduce racial segregation.
  • Postal banking to increase availability of services.
  • Prioritize voting rights protections.
  • Universal broadband.
  • Macy's committed to buying 15% of products from black-owned businesses. Provide incentives for others to do the same.
  • Revisit language. Black and white accentuates differences. They are opposite and not true skin colors. We are all shades of brown.




    • Class

  • Adopt a guaranteed income sometimes referred to as Universal Basic Income (UBI).
  • Institute a salary ratio of 10 to 1 from top to bottom of company. A CEO could make no more than 10 times the salary of the lowest paid employee.
  • Provide universal health care.
  • Wages must be sufficient to provide access to affordable housing.
  • Change inheritance laws to cap inheritance at a maximum of one million per household.
  • Remove inheritance tax, so that lower income people are not disproportionately affected.



    • Gender

  • Enforce equal pay.
  • Provide affordable child care for anyone who wants to work.
  • Promote gender education programs to increase support for equality.
  • Evaluate gender roles within families. Provide education to promote family roles based on strengths rather than gender.
  • Encourage girls and women to take STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) classes and pursue STEM careers.
  • Provide incentives for companies such as Macy's to purchase 50% of products from female owned companies.



    • Sexuality

  • Provide health care and counseling services for transgender individuals.
  • Provide affordable surgery for individuals who wish to transition.
  • Strengthen anti-discrimination laws for LGBTQIA people.
  • Increase budget for anti-discrimination education.
  • Increase penalties for hate crimes.
  • Recognize that gender is not binary.



    • Ability

  • Enforce Americans with Disabilties Act (ADA).
  • Provide funding to develop and use technology to increase ability.
  • Encourage people to participate in activities to the best of their ability. For example, a studio in New York teaches children with Multiple Sclerosis, Muscular Dystrophy, etc. to dance.
  • Improve access to equipment to help improve health, such as walking assistance machines.
  • A museum in the Netherlands recreates famous paintings in 3D for people who are blind. Universally implement similar programs.

    • Language

  • Begin teaching a second language in pre-school. Language is easiest to learn at this age.
  • Make fluency in a second language a requirement for high school graduation in the United States.
  • Use voice to voice translation applications on phones to improve communication.
  • Improve education regarding the need for knowing a second language.
  • Ensure government and other common forms are available in multiple languages.
  • Make translation readily available through technology or personnel.
